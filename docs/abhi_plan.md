Branch 1: Online Mode (The "Ghost Operator")
This branch triggers when the user has mobile data or Wi-Fi. It uses the Antigravity manager surface to orchestrate multiple Gemini agents in parallel.
The Example Scenario:
A user is walking home late, feels threatened, and discretely says the safeword: "Hey, my battery is at 2 percent."
1. The Orchestrator Agent (Antigravity Manager / Interactions API)
Role: The brain of the operation. It sits in the background, ingesting sensor data and audio.
Action: It hears the safeword, instantly classifies it as a "Code Red," and simultaneously spawns the three sub-agents below without ever turning on the phone screen.
2. The Verification Agent (Omni / Gemini 3.5 Flash)
Role: Ambient context gathering.
Action: It leaves the microphone open and processes the continuous raw audio stream. It detects the sound of fast footsteps or an aggressive voice approaching, confirms the escalation, and streams this context back to the Orchestrator to upgrade the threat level.
3. The Action Agent (Gemini 3.5 Flash via Computer Use)
Role: Zero-UI app manipulation.
Action: (This is what you will demo via Playwright) It completely bypasses the need for the user to open an app. It invisibly spins up Uber or Ola in the background, reads the current GPS location, finds the nearest police station or safe zone, and books the cab.
4. The Comms Agent (Gemini 3.1 Flash Live)
Role: External crisis communication.
Action: It initiates a live, bi-directional API voice call to the user’s emergency contacts. It uses a calm, synthesized voice: "Hello, this is Kavach. Abhishek has triggered a silent alarm at [Location]. A cab has been dispatched to their location. I will patch you into their live microphone now."
Branch 2: Offline Mode (The "Dark Survival" Flow)
This branch triggers when mobile data drops, or the attacker jams the signal. Everything shifts to the local device.
The Tech Stack: Gemma 4 (E2B Model). Google released this in April 2026 with a mobile-quantized (QAT) footprint of under 1GB. Crucially, it processes raw audio natively without needing a cloud-based speech-to-text encoder.
1. The Edge Sentinel (Gemma 4 Audio)
Role: Continuous offline monitoring.
Action: Since it doesn't need the cloud, it continuously listens to raw audio waves. It can detect the acoustic signature of glass breaking, a struggle, or offline safewords with near-zero latency.
2. The "Crazy" Feature: Ghost Camouflage & Cellular Beaconing
If the user is cornered and completely offline, Gemma 4 triggers the ultimate defensive protocol. Here is the wild feature you should build your mind map around:
The "Bricked Screen" Illusion: Gemma 4 executes a hardware-level macro that instantly drops the screen brightness to absolute zero and disables the power button UI. If an attacker grabs the phone, it looks completely dead or broken. They are likely to ignore it or throw it in a bag rather than demanding a passcode.
Invisible SOS Beacon: While playing dead, Gemma 4 leverages basic cell tower triangulation. Even without a data plan or internet, a phone can send SMS. Gemma 4 brute-forces a continuous loop of SMS text messages to emergency contacts containing encrypted GPS coordinates.
Acoustic Counter-Measure: If the phone's accelerometer detects it is being violently grabbed or thrown, Gemma 4 bypasses the volume limits and blasts a 120dB high-frequency localized distress tone out of the bottom speaker. It is a frequency specifically designed to disorient and cause physical ear discomfort, forcing the attacker to drop the device, while simultaneously drawing attention from a block away.
